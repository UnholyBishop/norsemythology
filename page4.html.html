Norse Deities

Gods and Goddesses names

  1. Odin- Odin was the king of the Asier, the principal race of Norse gods. He was considered the father of all the gods and was primarily associated with magic, wisdom, war, poetry, and the runic alphabet
  2. Idun- A beautiful Goddess with long golden hair, she is the Goddess of spring and eternal youth, she guards the apples of youth in Norse mythology.
  3. Thor- Norse mythology, Thor is an Aesir god who personifies strength and fertility. Generally depicted with red hair and a red beard, he was prominent throughout the history of the Germanic tribes. His popularity reached a peak during the Viking Age.
  4. Tyr- With his story shrouded in mystery and seemingly eclipsed by that of Thor and Odin, Tyr is not a well-known Norse god. What is known about Tyr is that he is a god of battle, courage, and swords. But more than just being involved in war, Tyr is also heavily involved in matters of law, justice, honor, and oaths. He also has a large respect for authority.
  5. Freya- Shee is the God of fertilitys he is also associated with prosperity, wealth and a good harvest.
  6. Frigg- Frigg is a sorcerer and associated with love, marriage, fertility, and motherhood, she also has the power of prophecy and yet she never reveals what she knows about the future.
  7. Baldur- Balder is so bright that light shines from him. Balder is the God of light and purity in Norse mythology and all the Gods praise him for his beauty. Balder is so beautiful that even the most beautiful flowers bows before him, as he walks past them. Balder is also one of the wisest of the Aesir and is the most fairest-spoken and most gracious.
  8. Heimdall- Heimdall is the guardian of the rainbow bridge Bifrost which is the only way into Asgard (the home of the Gods) and connecting it with Midgard (the home of man) in Norse mythology.
  9. Bragi- Bragi is the God of both poetry and music in Norse mythology, he is the son of the God Odin and the giantess Gunnlod. Bragi is very wise, and he is known for his wisdom he is very creative with words and he also has the most knowledge of poems and songs.
  10. Njord- God of the wind, seafarers, coasts, inland waters and wealth, he is a member of the Vanir. When the war between the Vanir and the Aesir ended, Njord and two others were sent to the Aesir as a token of truce, in return, the Aesir sent Honir and Mimir to the Vanir.
